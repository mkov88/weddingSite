Travel

For those of you who are traveling from out-of-town, we are so grateful that you are making the trip to (ILM)Wilmington for our wedding celebration. Feel free to use the map below to search for flights (Directions > Airplane Icon). Flying into (MYR)Myrtle Beach, SC or (RDU)Raleigh, NC are other travel options. The flights are typically cheaper, however there will be about an hour and a half to two hour drive to Wilmington. Please contact us if you have any concerns or questions about travel.

Dope Things to Do When You Livin Dat Salt Life

Downtown Wilmington/Historic District
Head to Front Street to be right in the middle of many local restaurants and shops. While you’re downtown, make sure to walk along the river to check out a great view! Some of our favorite dining places are The Copper Penny, Fork ’N’ Cork, and Beer Barrio. If you need a sweet treat, go to Kilwin's for some amazing ice cream and fudge. Find out about local tours (historic, Hollywood sites, ghost walks, etc.)  from the Visitor Information Center located on the corner of Market and S. Water Streets.

Wrightsville Beach
You definitely shouldn't come to Wilmington without checking out the beach!  Wrightsville Beach is about 5-10 minutes from the hotels. It is pay-to-park, but you can always catch an Uber to avoid parking costs. Some places to eat are Tower 7, Oceanic, Sweet and Savory (breakfast), and Ceviche. Our favorite brunch place is the Famous Toastery.

link to site link to directions

Lumina Station
Within 5 minutes of the hotels are Lumina Station and Mayfaire Town Center. Check out Lumina Station for some local shopping and dining options. Some of our favorite restaurants in Lumina Station are David’s Deli & Restaurant and Halligans Public House. If you’re looking for a nicer dinner try Port Land Grille.

link to site link to directions

Mayfaire Town Center
 If you are interested in doing some shopping or checking out a movie, Mayfaire is the place to go! Some of the stores include Bath and Body Works, Belk, New York and Company, and Barnes and Noble. There are plenty of places to grab a bite to eat, too!
